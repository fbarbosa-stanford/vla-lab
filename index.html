An interactive notebook on vision-language-action models — the policies that map a camera image and a language instruction straight to robot motion.

It is two things at once: a teaching tool that opens up how these models think, and a record of the VLAs I've trained — manipulation policies on the SO-101 arm, and a π₀.5 driving stack fine-tuned on NVIDIA autonomous-vehicle data. Everything below is interactive or links to real weights.

About this lab

I'm a CS undergrad at Stanford on the AI track, mostly thinking about reinforcement learning, agents, and robotics — and within robotics, about vision-language-action models and embodied reasoning. This lab collects that work in one place. Start with the Explorer to build intuition, then dig into the methods and the driving research.
