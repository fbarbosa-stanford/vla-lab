A research note: can the same flow-matching VLA recipe that places a Lego brick in a glass also learn to drive a car? I finetuned π₀.5 on NVIDIA autonomous-vehicle data and evaluated it open-loop on held-out clips.

The premise

π₀-style VLAs were built for manipulation, but nothing about the architecture is arm-specific: it maps a camera + a state vector to a chunk of future actions. Driving is the same shape — pixels and vehicle state in, motion out — just a different embodiment. So the experiment is to treat driving as one more robot and see how far the manipulation recipe carries.

1 · A driving policy from π₀.5

The base policy is π₀.5 finetuned on NVIDIA PhysicalAI autonomous-driving clips, framed in exactly the LeRobot schema the manipulation models use — just with a vehicle's observation and action spaces. Vision encoder frozen, flow-matching action head fine-tuned on the 50-clip set, with a held-out validation split.

   observation.images.front  =  224 x 224 x 3        (front-facing camera)
   observation.state   (8)   =  [ pos_x pos_y pos_z  vel_x vel_y vel_z  yaw  speed ]
   action              (3)   =  ego-frame [ dx  dy  dz ]  per 0.1s step   ( 10 Hz )
the policy predicts where the vehicle moves next, in its own frame, as a short trajectory
